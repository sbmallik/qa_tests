Critics' Picks: Best film festival

Matt Lankes/IFC

Phoenix Film Festival

website

by Bill Goodykoontz, The Republic | azcentral.com

Our state is home to a surprisingly rich and varied number of film festivals. The Sedona Film Festival is always fun — can't beat the location — and the Scottsdale International Film Festival has really grown both in numbers and in terms of the profile of its films. But the Phoenix Film Festival is still the biggie. It brings in good films — "Boyhood," a likely best-picture nominee at the Oscars, made its Arizona debut there this year — and talent. And there's a good after-party, if you want to sip wine with a director. And directors do show up; this is known as a filmmaker-friendly festival. There are also educational and entertainment programs throughout the festival's run.
